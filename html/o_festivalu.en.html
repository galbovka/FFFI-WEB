About

Film Festival of Faculty of Informatics is the result of work done by students of Masaryk University, which, during two semesters, in the courses PV110 a PV113 get through overall film making process, often without any experience beforehand. Individually or in small teams, they form their ideas into screenplays, they film material, cut it and postproduce required parts and they become screenwriters, directors, cameramen, soundmen, producers, actors and others.

Finished films of diverse genres and technical approaches are publically shown on May's festival in FI MU to over 600 viewers and the best ones are rewarded both by official jury and public poll. Winners of both awards get rewarded with numerous prizes and they also get a challenge trophy - a small statue of Filmobolos.

The first film festival was ran in 2001 out of joy from new options of digital video processing, from the needs identified in the making of "Všech pět pohromadě" CD and FI students' desire to create their own movies - express themselves both with words and images. In one semester, 10 films were made, from the initial author's idea, through screenplay, into production, digital cut and festival preparations. Success was unprecedented, as was said by the editor of student magazine Informagika: "The fact, that Informatics students created 10 enjoyable movies is as unbelievable as the chance that 10 English students write an operating system." Festival was repeated every year since and the quality bar and attendance are increasing every year.

Statute of the festival

Film festival „XV. Filmový festival Fakulty informatiky“ (further only FFFI) is a show organised and ran by Faculty of Informatics, Masaryk University in Brno. FFFI accepts, without restrictions in genre, only films with main authors being Masaryk University students or graduates. The goal of FFFI is to support academic production of short films.

No entrance fee.
